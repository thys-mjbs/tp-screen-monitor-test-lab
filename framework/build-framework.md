## Core Audience

Monitor buyers before or after purchase, office users checking display complaints, repair shop technicians, gamers calibrating new displays, and IT helpdesk staff triaging screen issues. High-volume searches with very low login tolerance — these users want the answer in under 5 seconds.

---

## Commercial Adjacency

Users testing dead pixels are frequently deciding whether to return a monitor, buy a new one, or book a repair. Ad inventory sits directly adjacent to monitor hardware, laptop screens, docking stations, display calibrators, and screen cleaners. This is not passive browsing — it is problem-solving right before a purchase or return decision. RPM will be lower than the QR toolkit (more consumer than B2B) but traffic ceiling is very high: whitescreen.online gets 800,000 monthly visits.

## Site-Specific Warnings

- Tool pages that are pure fullscreen panels (white screen, black screen, etc.) are thin by nature — compensate with strong FAQ content, clear use-case explanations, and internal links
- Stuck pixel fixers cannot guarantee results — FAQs must set realistic expectations
- Avoid making medical claims (e.g. "this will damage your eyes if used incorrectly")
- The "blue screen" tool will compete with the Windows blue screen of death search — angle the page clearly toward monitor testing, not error screens
